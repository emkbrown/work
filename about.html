ADVOCATE. ASPIRING UX DESIGNER. ON A MISSION TO USE TECH FOR GOOD.

I'm a junior at the University of Michigan's School of Information working toward my Bachelor of Science in Information with a concentration in UX Design. I've spent my formative years finding ways to combine my interests in art, education, and technology. In high school, I spent my time outside of programming class, orchestra, and theatre rehearsals designing t-shirts for my choir, performing in rock bands with my friends and family, and tutoring my peers in physics and calculus.

In college, I have spent much of my time working directly with high school students and incoming undergraduates as a teacher and mentor, while pursuing my creative interests on the side. I am committed to providing underserved and underrepresented students with educational and social support as they learn about careers in tech, and my approach focuses on personal connection as a pillar in encouraging students to explore their interests in programming and excel in a field without adequate representation. I have worked with U-M's Office of New Student Programs, Rise Up 4 CS, #BUILTBYGIRLS, and GEECS Outreach to empower young students to develop and pursue their aspirations in tech and otherwise.

I am looking to broaden my horizons as an aspiring UX Designer and learn more about industry. I believe tech can be used for good rather than evil, and I intend to hone my UX and front-end development skills with organizations who want to use and create tech to make the world a more sustainable, expressive, and equitable place.

I am an empathetic and strategic problem-solver with passions for creativity and human conncection. Check out some of my work and feel free to send me a message!
